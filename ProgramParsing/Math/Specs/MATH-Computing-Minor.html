Academic Plans and Requirements

Computer Science
Computing Minor
Printable Version

A Computing Minor is available to all students, except those enrolled in Software Engineering, Computing and Financial Management, and other plans involving Computer Science. Certain plans already including substantial computer science content may also be excluded. See the Bachelor of Computer Science and Bachelor of Mathematics Academic Plan Combinations page for more details about excluded plans.

Notes

  1. Most courses in the range CS 240-CS 299, CS 340-CS 398, CS 440-CS 498 are only available to CS majors, so upper-year CS courses taken toward this Minor will usually be in the range CS 200-CS 239, CS 300-CS 339, CS 400-CS 439.  
  2. A common route into upper-year CS courses is to take all of CS 115, CS 116, and CS 136. All three of these courses may count toward this Minor.
  3. Students cannot obtain both the Computer Science (CS) Minor and the Computing Minor.
